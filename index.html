Understanding Depression

What is Depression?

Depression is more than just feeling sad. It's a serious mental health condition that affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent sad, anxious, or "empty" mood
  • Feelings of hopelessness or pessimism
  • Loss of interest in hobbies and activities
  • Decreased energy or fatigue
  • Difficulty sleeping or oversleeping
  • Changes in appetite or weight

Treatment Options

  • Psychotherapy (Talk Therapy)
  • Medication (when prescribed)
  • Lifestyle changes
  • Support groups
  • Mindfulness and meditation

Understanding Anxiety

What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is your body's natural response to stress. While some anxiety is normal, excessive worry and fear that interfere with daily life may indicate an anxiety disorder.

Common Symptoms

  • Excessive worrying
  • Restlessness or feeling on edge
  • Rapid heartbeat or breathing
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Sleep problems
  • Physical symptoms like sweating or trembling

Coping Strategies

  • Practice deep breathing exercises
  • Use grounding techniques
  • Regular exercise
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol
  • Maintain a consistent sleep schedule

Managing Stress

Understanding Stress

Stress is your body's response to pressure. While some stress can be motivating, chronic stress can negatively impact your physical and mental health.

Signs of Stress

  • Feeling overwhelmed or frustrated
  • Difficulty relaxing
  • Changes in eating or sleeping habits
  • Physical symptoms like headaches
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Increased irritability

Management Techniques

  • Mindfulness and meditation
  • Regular exercise
  • Time management strategies
  • Setting boundaries
  • Progressive muscle relaxation

Self-Care Practices

What is Self-Care?

Self-care means taking time to pay attention to yourself, both physically and mentally. It's about making your health and well-being a priority.

Physical Self-Care

  • Regular exercise and movement
  • Healthy eating habits
  • Getting enough sleep
  • Regular medical check-ups
  • Taking breaks when needed

Mental Self-Care

  • Practicing mindfulness
  • Journaling
  • Setting healthy boundaries
  • Engaging in hobbies
  • Seeking therapy when needed
